TimeLine for the
Robots & Foundations Universe

Isaac Asimov

My love of science fiction started in the early 80's when I'd sneak into my school's library scouring for good books to read in Spanish translation. It wasn't until much, much later that I discovered the Robots and Empire novels and stories.

Even later on, as I was reading Prelude To Foundation that I saw a reading list tying together all the robots, empire and Foundation novels together. It's over a million words.

The final piece of the puzzle is the timeline below. I first saw it on David Brin's Foundation's Triump. It puts all the writings for those stories in chronological content.

Notes:

Timeline compiled by Attila Torkos, 1998. I transcribed it from the appendix to Foundation's Triumph.

Books listed in color are chapters inside a book. The original timeline doesn't list the book, just the name of the chapter or short story

    3700 C.E Death of Han Fastolfe. Population of Solaria vanishes. Daneel Olivaw and Giskard Relentlov formulate the Zeroth Law of Robotics, to override original Three Laws.

    Giskard provides Daneel with telepathic abilities. Amadiro and allies turn on amplifiers to make the Earth radioactive and inhabitable. Giskard permits this for Zeroth Law reasons, in order to encourage human dispersal, then dies because of First Law conflicts.

    The Great Diaspora (final emigration) begins. A majority of robots split into two camps. Giskardians led by Daneel follow the new Zeroth Law religion. Calvinians think this an outrage.Robotic civil war ensues, mostly unseen by humans fleeing poisoned Earth.

    Meanwhile robot ships operating under Auroran programming cruise the galaxy ahead of the spreading Settlers, terraforming and preparing planets for colonization. Meme entities will later claim that this devastated existingraces. Memes escape to the Galactic Core.
